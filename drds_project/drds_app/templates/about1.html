About Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes complication that affects the eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye (retina).

The primary risk factor for diabetic retinopathy is diabetes. Controlling your blood sugar levels and managing your diabetes effectively can help prevent or slow the progression of diabetic retinopathy.

Symptoms of Diabetic Retinopathy

  • Blurry vision
  • Difficulty seeing at night
  • Seeing dark spots or floaters
  • Sudden loss of vision

Prevention and Control

Managing diabetes effectively can help prevent diabetic retinopathy. Here are a few steps you can take:

  • Regular eye exams
  • Controlling blood sugar levels
  • Maintaining healthy blood pressure and cholesterol levels

Treatment Options

If diabetic retinopathy is detected early, it can be treated with various methods, including:

  • Laser treatment
  • Injections of medication into the eye
  • Surgical procedures in severe cases
